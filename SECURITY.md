# Safety and Security

## UV Light Safety

This project uses 365nm UV-A LED strips. UV-A radiation poses health risks with prolonged or direct exposure:

- **Eye damage:** Direct exposure to UV-A light can cause photokeratitis (corneal sunburn) and may contribute to long-term eye conditions. Never look directly at the UV LEDs when powered on.
- **Skin burns:** Extended UV-A exposure can cause skin irritation and burns. Minimize skin exposure during operation.
- **Photosensitizing materials:** Some chemicals used in alternative photography (e.g., dichromates in gum bichromate) are hazardous. Follow material safety data sheets (MSDS) for all chemicals used.

### Recommended Precautions

- Wear UV-blocking safety glasses (rated for 365nm) when the device is powered on
- Keep the box lid closed during exposure
- Do not operate the device with the lid open or removed
- Work in a well-ventilated area, especially when using photographic chemicals
- Keep out of reach of children

## Electrical Safety

- Use only the specified 12V DC power supply
- Ensure all wiring connections are secure and properly insulated
- Disconnect power before modifying wiring or components
- Do not operate with exposed or damaged wiring
- Keep the device away from water and moisture
- The relay module switches 12V DC; verify relay ratings match your LED strip current draw

## Reporting Security Issues

If you discover a security vulnerability in the firmware or documentation, please open a GitHub issue.
